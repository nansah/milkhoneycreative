Web Design

Boujee Banker

A full conversion-focused redesign for a personal finance platform — rebuilt from the ground up with sharper user flows, a bold visual identity, and architecture engineered to sell.

Services UX Audit, Website Redesign, Conversion Strategy
Category High-Conversion Website
Audience Women & Personal Finance

The Challenge

The Boujee Banker is a personal finance platform for women, built around digital products — ebooks, courses, and live webinars. The brand had strong social media presence but the website wasn't converting visitors into buyers. The existing site had critical UX problems that were actively costing revenue.

  • No logo or brand name visible on the homepage — a fundamental trust failure
  • The book page navigation disappeared entirely, stranding users mid-purchase
  • No clear calls to action — visitors couldn't determine what to do or buy first
  • Visual design was drab, inconsistent with the brand's bold Instagram aesthetic
  • Webinar was buried with zero value proposition on the homepage

The Approach

I started with a full UX audit — documenting broken flows, accessibility issues, and missed conversion opportunities. Then I conducted a competitive analysis across finance coaches including Patrice Washington and Anthony O'Neal to understand what high-converting platforms in this space looked like.

The redesign prioritized three things: conversion architecture (every page has a clear next action), visual alignment with the brand's vibrant, feminine aesthetic, and a persistent navigation that never abandons the user mid-journey.

The Result

Up Digital product sales post-launch
Higher Webinar sign-up rates
Zero Broken navigation flows remaining

The redesign directly improved digital product sales and webinar sign-ups. By fixing the fundamental UX failures and aligning the site's visual language with the brand's audience, the platform began working as hard as the content it was built to sell.
