Web Design

House of Faith Ministries

A modern digital home for a growing ministry — clean, fast, and content-rich, built to serve both their existing congregation and visitors discovering them for the first time.

Services Website Design & Development
Category Organizational Website
Focus Content Architecture, Navigation UX

The Challenge

House of Faith Ministries was a growing ministry without a digital presence that matched their reach. They needed a website that could serve multiple audiences simultaneously — longtime members looking for sermon archives and event updates, and first-time visitors trying to understand who they are.

  • No centralized hub for sermons, events, and ministry resources
  • Navigation needed to accommodate diverse content categories without overwhelming new visitors
  • The site had to feel welcoming and accessible — not corporate or cold
  • Fast load times were critical to serve users across varying device and connection types

The Approach

I designed the information architecture first — mapping out the different user journeys (first-time visitor, regular attendee, remote congregation member) and building navigation that worked intuitively for all three. The visual design was kept clean and warm, prioritizing readability and clarity over visual complexity.

Content sections were structured to surface the highest-priority information — upcoming events, sermon library, ways to connect — without requiring users to hunt for what they need.

The Result

One Centralized hub for all ministry content
Dual UX optimized for both new visitors and members

The result is a welcoming, fast digital experience that works equally well for a first-time visitor who found them on Google and a longtime member checking for Sunday's service notes.
